Sampler

Switch to Scripting

The Sampler is an instrument that has a collection of audio samples to play and will play them at different rates to change the pitch for different notes. A list of keyzones define what samples play when what notes are hit. With this you can create a percussive instrument where each note is a different sample or create a melodic instrument where a given sample is stretched to play different notes.

Piano Usage

Use the graphical piano to play sampler notes and test out sounds.

Keyzone Usage

The keyzones define what samples are started when a note is played and how much to pitch shift it. Each keyzone has an Audio Clip, an Audio Mixer Group, a Root Key and a Key Range.

  • Audio Clip - The audio clip to play for this keyzone.
  • Audio Mixer Group - What mixer to send the audio clip to.
  • Root Key - What key to change the pitch from. The Root Key should match the pitch of your recored sample.
  • Key Range - The range of notes that this key range is active in. If a note is played within this range, the Audio Clip assosiated with this keyzon will play.
When a note is played, the Sampler looks at all the keyzones. If the note is within the key range of one of the keyzones, the sample of that keyzone is played and pitch shifted.

◆ Num Voices

Total number of concurrently playing sounds from this Sampler (polyphony). Increase this if your voices are cutting out from new voices coming in.

◆ Velocity Tracking

How much the velocity of a note on event affects the volume of the samples. 0.0 for no effect. If 1.0, max velocity sets the volume of the clip to 1.0 and min velocity sets the volume of the clip to 0.0.
